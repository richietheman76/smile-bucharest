If you've been quoted £2,500–£3,500 for a single dental implant by your UK dentist, you're probably wondering: is there a better option? The answer is yes — and it's closer than you might think.

Romania has become one of Europe's leading dental tourism destinations. Bucharest in particular is home to dozens of world-class dental clinics staffed by EU-trained specialists who use the same implant brands and materials as the best clinics in London, Munich or Paris — but charge a fraction of the price.

This guide covers everything you need to know about getting dental implants in Romania in 2025: exact costs, what's included, how the process works, and what real patients have saved.

If you need 4 implants: UK cost = £10,000–£14,000. Romania cost = €1,400–€2,600. Even after flights and a week's hotel, you save £7,000–£10,000.

2. What's Included in the Price?

At a quality clinic in Bucharest, the implant price should always include:

  • Initial consultation with the implantologist
  • Panoramic X-ray and/or 3D CT scan
  • The implant post (titanium fixture)
  • The healing abutment
  • The final restoration crown (zirconia or porcelain)
  • All local anaesthesia
  • Post-operative care kit and instructions
  • Written 5-year guarantee

At SmileBucharest, we also include airport transfer (both ways) and remote follow-up with your treating dentist after you return home.

4. Implant Systems: Which Should You Choose?

The implant brand affects both the price and the long-term outcome. Here's what to know:

Osstem (Korean)

The world's best-selling implant system. Used in over 100 countries, with an extensive clinical track record. An excellent choice for most patients — the price difference vs Straumann does not reflect a meaningful quality gap for standard cases.

Nobel Biocare (Swiss)

A premium system widely used in the UK and US. Particularly good for complex cases, immediate-load protocols (same-day teeth), and All-on-4 procedures. Many UK patients specifically request Nobel Biocare when coming to Romania.

Straumann (Swiss)

Widely considered the gold standard. Straumann implants have the longest clinical studies behind them and are favoured for their surface technology, which promotes faster osseointegration. Recommended for medically complex patients or those who want the absolute best.

5. The Treatment Process: Step by Step

Getting dental implants abroad is simpler than most people expect. Here's how the process works at SmileBucharest:

  1. Free remote consultation — Send your X-rays or photos via WhatsApp. We review them and send a detailed treatment plan and price quote within 24 hours.
  2. Book your trip — Once you confirm, we help you plan flights and accommodation. We'll book your treatment dates around your schedule.
  3. Arrival & initial appointment — Our driver picks you up from the airport. Day 1 is a clinical examination, final X-rays, and treatment planning.
  4. Implant placement — Usually done on Day 2–3. Under local anaesthesia, the titanium implant is placed into the jawbone. The procedure itself takes 45–90 minutes per implant.
  5. Healing period — The implant integrates with the bone over 3–6 months. You return home after your initial stay. A temporary crown may be fitted in the meantime.
  6. Return visit for the crown — 3–6 months later, you return for 2–3 days to have your final zirconia crown fitted. This is a quick appointment with no surgery involved.
  7. Ongoing support — Your treating dentist is available for remote follow-up. Your written guarantee covers the implant for 5 years.

6. How Long Do I Need to Stay?

For a standard single implant:

  • First visit: 4–5 days (consultation + implant placement + initial healing check)
  • Second visit: 2–3 days (crown fitting, 3–6 months later)

For multiple implants or All-on-4, the first visit may extend to 5–7 days. Many patients combine the trip with a few days of sightseeing in Bucharest — the city has excellent restaurants, a vibrant Old Town, and affordable prices across the board.

7. Is It Safe to Get Implants in Romania?

This is the most common concern — and completely understandable. Here's the honest answer:

Romania is a full EU member state. Dental clinics are regulated by the Romanian College of Dentists to standards equivalent to those in France, Germany, or the Netherlands. Romanian dental education is among the best in Europe — the faculty at Bucharest's "Carol Davila" University is internationally respected.

Many Romanian dentists have also completed postgraduate training in Germany, France, Austria, or the UK. It's common to find an implantologist in Bucharest who has worked in a London or Berlin clinic.

⚠️ How to Choose a Safe Clinic

Look for: EU-registered dentists with verifiable credentials, documented use of brand-name implant systems, before/after photos, English-speaking staff, written guarantees, and transparent pricing. Avoid any clinic that gives you a quote without reviewing X-rays.
